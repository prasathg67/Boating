Mangrove

If you want to have a unique experience, then you should not miss the thrill of exploring the dense Mangrove Forest in Puducherry. This forest is lined along the Thengaithittu Estuary and offers visual treats in the form of dense mangrove forests. hiding the water beneath them. we PY BOATS ADVENDTURE will help explore the entire forest. This Mangrove Forest has unique formations of trees that resemble a tunnel through which your boat will pass. The surreal beauty of this forest is worthy of capturing in a camera.

Fishing Harbour

The Union Territory of Pondicherry has a coast line of 45 kms and a continental shelf of 675 sq./kms. Located in the Ariyankuppam River at Thengaithittu. There are 50 fishing hamlets of varying sizes and generally the living conditions are far from comfortable. It has been proposed to establish a Fishing Harbour at Pondicherry in the year 1984. Some of the most popular fishing villages in Pondicherry include Thengaithittu, Vaithikuppam Vambakeerapalayam, Veerampattinam, Kuruchikuppam and Nallavadu surrounded by it.

Communal Roosting (Birds)

Many people are not aware of the mangroves in Thengaithittu Pondicherry and how important these mangroves are in preserving the biodiversity of the area. However, the area of mangroves has not been studied in detail so far for its importance as a bird-roosting site. Over the years, a number of birds started arriving in the area. A number of species of birds are living here in harmony and exhibiting what is known as "communal roosting," where several birds choose to roost together in the same area.

Arikamedu

A historical site and a place for all history-buffs located on the outskirts of Puducherry, Arikamedu is a bridge between Roman and Indian architecture. An archaeological site maintained by the Archaeological Society of India, As you enter the site, you will come across ancient columns, walls, and mortars standing tall along the Ariyankuppam river with dilapidated French Jesuit Mission buildings dating back to the 18th century. Interestingly, Arikamedu had an urban settlement between 1000 BC to 100 AD.
